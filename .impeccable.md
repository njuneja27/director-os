## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are product owners, founders, or technical directors steering one software product with the help of AI agents. They are trying to stay at the level of product intent, approvals, testing, and strategic tradeoffs rather than issue grooming and code-review busywork.

### Brand Personality
Calm, exacting, executive. The interface should feel trustworthy, editorial, and operationally sharp rather than flashy or theatrical. It should create confidence and a sense of control.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first and text-forward, with a restrained newsroom or briefing-room feel. Favor warm neutrals, dark ink tones, strong hierarchy, and dense but readable information layouts. Anti-references are generic AI dashboards, glowing dark-mode control rooms, metric-card grids, and decorative SaaS chrome.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with judgment, not ornament.
2. Present recommendations and consequences before detail.
3. Keep the interface quiet enough for daily use.
4. Prefer structured lists and editorial layouts over dashboard filler.
5. Make state transitions obvious without relying on gimmicky motion.
